Full-Funnel Demand Generation Architecture

The end-to-end framework I use to build demand gen programs from first touch through revenue attribution.

AwarenessBlog, Social, Webinars
CaptureGated Content, Forms
SegmentBehavioral Signal
NurtureTrack-Specific Email Journey
ScoreExplicit + Implicit Scoring
HandoffMQL → SDR (24hr SLA)
Feedback LoopSales Sync → Refine

Awareness → Capture

Top-funnel content drives broad awareness. Gated assets like benchmark reports, checklists, and regulatory guides capture leads and signal intent. The gate isn't just lead capture; it's the segmentation trigger.

Segment → Nurture

Behavioral signals from gated content route leads into segment-specific nurture tracks. Each track has a different goal, different content depth, and different CTAs reflecting where that buyer is in their journey.

Score → Handoff

Two-part scoring: behavioral engagement + demographic fit. MQL threshold triggers automated CRM alert to SDR with 24-hour outreach SLA. Sales gets context: what the lead engaged with, what segment they're in.

Feedback Loop → Recycle

Bi-weekly syncs with sales to review conversion data and recalibrate scoring thresholds. Cold leads enter a 60-90 day re-engagement track rather than aging out of the database.

Sample Campaign Strategy

B2B Healthcare Demand Generation Campaign

A sample end-to-end demand generation campaign built for a healthcare organization targeting health plan and provider audiences. This reflects how I approach campaign strategy from first audience insight through revenue attribution.

The Starting Point

A regulated B2B healthcare SaaS company serving health plan and provider organizations. The goal: build a scalable demand generation program that generates qualified pipeline and attributes marketing activity to revenue. No playbook exists. Infrastructure needs to be built before campaigns can scale.

Audience Segmentation

Before any campaign goes live, I identify distinct buyer segments based on use case and pain point. Treating different audiences the same hurts performance and wastes budget.

Segment 1

Health Plan Decision Makers

VP and Director level · Quality and Risk functions

Focused on compliance, CMS performance, and cost management. Needs data-backed ROI and regulatory credibility before engaging.

LinkedIn · Email Nurture · Webinars · Case Studies

Segment 2

Provider and Clinical Operations

Operations and Quality leadership · Provider groups

Focused on workflow efficiency, clinical outcomes, and staff burden. Responds to peer validation and practical implementation content.

Google Search · LinkedIn · Peer Case Studies · Events

Segment 3

Executive and Strategic Buyers

C-suite and SVP level · Final decision authority

Focused on strategic impact, competitive positioning, and enterprise risk. Responds to thought leadership and high-level ROI framing.

LinkedIn · Executive Content · Industry Events · ABM

Campaign Phases

01

Foundation

Weeks 1 to 6

  • Audit existing database, channel performance, and content gaps
  • Define audience segments and ICP with sales and RevOps input
  • Build lead scoring model combining behavioral and firmographic signals
  • Set up CRM workflows, UTM tracking, and attribution framework
  • Establish MQL definition and sales handoff SLA
  • Develop gated top-funnel content aligned to each segment
  • Launch paid media with tight audience targeting and conversion tracking
02

Activation

Weeks 6 to 16

  • Launch segment-specific nurture tracks with escalating content depth
  • Mid-funnel gated assets live: eBooks, webinars, benchmark reports
  • Paid media optimized toward lead quality not just volume
  • A/B testing on landing page headlines, CTAs, and form length
  • First MQLs passed to sales with full engagement context
  • Bi-weekly sales sync to review lead quality and adjust scoring
  • Content-to-pipeline attribution tracking begins
03

Optimization

Ongoing

  • Monthly full-funnel review: pipeline contribution by channel
  • Scoring model recalibrated based on what actually converts to revenue
  • Re-engagement track for leads that went cold at 60 to 90 days
  • Content production prioritized by pipeline attribution data
  • Budget reallocated toward highest CAC-to-LTV efficiency channels
  • Quarterly strategy review and roadmap refresh
  • Executive reporting framework updated with trends and recommendations

Channel Mix Philosophy

35% Paid Media LinkedIn for B2B targeting by title and company. Google Search for high-intent queries. Optimized toward MQL quality not impression volume.
30% Content and SEO Gated assets drive lead capture and segmentation signals. SEO compounds over time. Every content piece mapped to funnel stage and buyer segment.
25% Email and Nurture Segment-specific tracks with behavioral triggers. Scoring advances with each engagement. Re-engagement sequences for cold leads at 60 to 90 days.
10% Events and Webinars High-intent channel for healthcare audiences. Webinar attendance is a strong scoring signal. Used to accelerate mid-funnel progression.

Measurement Framework

Pipeline Metrics

  • Marketing-generated pipeline by channel and segment
  • MQL volume, quality score, and conversion rate
  • MQL to SQL conversion rate
  • Marketing-attributed revenue and influenced revenue

Efficiency Metrics

  • Cost per MQL by channel
  • CAC relative to customer LTV
  • Conversion rate at each funnel stage
  • Content-to-pipeline attribution by asset

What I Would Not Measure

  • Impressions or reach in isolation
  • Website sessions without conversion context
  • Social followers or engagement rate
  • Email open rate as a primary success metric
