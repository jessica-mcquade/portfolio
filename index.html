Demand Generation

B2B Healthcare | Medicare Advantage

Building a Demand Gen Function from Zero

  • 266% Pipeline growth YoY
  • $9.5M Marketing-attributed revenue
  • 43% MQL-to-SQL improvement

The Situation

Joined as the first dedicated digital marketing hire at a healthcare SaaS company serving Medicare Advantage plans. No infrastructure existed: no CRM workflows, no lead scoring, no campaign playbooks, no reporting framework. The company was generating some leads but had no system for qualifying, nurturing, or converting them.

The Strategic Decision

Rather than launching campaigns immediately into a broken system, I made the deliberate decision to build the foundation first. Running paid media into a funnel with no qualification logic would generate noise, not pipeline. The infrastructure had to come before the volume.

What I Built

Developed a two-part lead scoring model combining explicit demographic fit (title, company size, vertical) with behavioral intent signals. Built segmented nurture workflows in Pardot for two distinct buyer segments: risk adjustment and quality improvement — each with escalating content depth and a defined MQL threshold. Created standardized campaign playbooks, reporting templates, and a full-funnel attribution framework. Built an SEO strategy in a regulated healthcare environment, increasing organic traffic 32% YoY. Managed one associate and directed agency partners on paid media execution.

The Outcome

Grew marketing-generated pipeline from $6.8M to $25.1M — 266% growth — and drove $9.5M in marketing-attributed revenue. MQL-to-SQL conversion improved 43% after rebuilding the scoring model in partnership with sales and RevOps. The infrastructure scaled beyond my direct involvement because it was built as a system, not a collection of one-off campaigns.

Paid Media

National Medical Events Brand

Driving 13.7x ROAS on Flagship Campaign

  • 13.7x Return on ad spend
  • 3 Channels optimized
  • ↑32% Landing page conversion lift

The Situation

Led digital strategy for a national medical conference brand targeting physicians and clinicians. Inherited existing paid media programs across Google, LinkedIn, and Meta with inconsistent performance benchmarks and limited attribution visibility between ad spend and actual registrations.

The Strategic Decision

Shifted from a volume-first paid strategy to a conversion-focused approach, rebuilding campaign architecture around audience segmentation, landing page alignment, and full-funnel attribution. Identified that a significant portion of spend was going to broad audiences with low registration intent and reallocated toward higher-intent signals.

What I Built

Rebuilt campaign structure across all three channels with tighter audience segmentation by specialty and role. Partnered with agency to optimize landing page messaging, aligning ad copy directly to page headlines to reduce friction and improve quality score. Established KPI dashboards and executive reporting frameworks connecting ad performance to downstream revenue. Implemented consistent UTM tracking and conversion event architecture for cleaner attribution.

The Outcome

Achieved 13.7x ROAS on flagship campaigns. Landing page conversion rate improved 32% through messaging alignment and UX friction reduction. Executive reporting framework gave leadership clear visibility into performance for the first time, enabling faster budget reallocation decisions.

SEO & Organic Growth

B2B Healthcare: SEO & CRO

Building an SEO Strategy in a Regulated Healthcare Space

  • 32% Organic traffic growth YoY
  • $0 Dedicated SEO budget
  • ↑ Domain authority improvement

The Situation

Audited the company's digital presence and found zero SEO strategy in place: wrong site structure, no keyword targeting, poor metadata, and content that wasn't optimized for search at all. In a regulated healthcare space, this is particularly high-stakes. One non-compliant keyword or claim can create legal and regulatory risk, and standard SEO playbooks don't translate directly.

The Strategic Decision

Decided to build a real, compliant SEO strategy rather than patch individual pages starting with a full technical and content audit, then developing a keyword framework specifically designed for the regulatory constraints of Medicare and health plan marketing.

What I Built

Built a keyword strategy around terms our actual buyers were searching, balancing search volume with regulatory safety. Created a content brief template that allowed subject matter experts to contribute accurate, compliant information efficiently. Directed agency partners on technical fixes including site structure, metadata, and page speed. Established an editorial calendar for consistent content production without overburdening internal teams.

The Outcome

32% year-over-year growth in organic traffic in a regulated healthcare space, with no dedicated SEO budget and a one-person team. Built as a documented, scalable system that continued generating results beyond the initial build. Demonstrated that organic acquisition is viable even in highly regulated healthcare environments when the strategy is built around compliance from the start.

Full-Funnel Demand Generation Architecture

The end-to-end framework I use to build demand gen programs from first touch through revenue attribution.

AwarenessBlog, Social, Webinars
CaptureGated Content, Forms
SegmentBehavioral Signal
NurtureTrack-Specific Email Journey
ScoreExplicit + Implicit Scoring
HandoffMQL → SDR (24hr SLA)
Feedback LoopSales Sync → Refine

Awareness → Capture

Top-funnel content drives broad awareness. Gated assets like benchmark reports, checklists, and regulatory guides capture leads and signal intent. The gate isn't just lead capture; it's the segmentation trigger.

Segment → Nurture

Behavioral signals from gated content route leads into segment-specific nurture tracks. Each track has a different goal, different content depth, and different CTAs reflecting where that buyer is in their journey.

Score → Handoff

Two-part scoring: behavioral engagement + demographic fit. MQL threshold triggers automated CRM alert to SDR with 24-hour outreach SLA. Sales gets context: what the lead engaged with, what segment they're in.

Feedback Loop → Recycle

Bi-weekly syncs with sales to review conversion data and recalibrate scoring thresholds. Cold leads enter a 60-90 day re-engagement track rather than aging out of the database.

Sample Campaign Strategy

Medicare Market Entry Campaign Plan

A hypothetical patient acquisition strategy for a value-based primary care organization entering a new urban Medicare market, built around how this population actually makes healthcare decisions.

Context & Business Model

Value-based primary care for Medicare-eligible patients. Revenue model: per-member-per-month (PMPM) payments from Medicare Advantage plans based on attributed patients. Patient acquisition is not a marketing metric, it's' the primary revenue driver. Every attributed patient represents recurring monthly revenue. CAC is only meaningful relative to patient LTV.

Audience Segmentation

Segment 1

Relatively Healthy, Digitally Engaged

Ages 65–72 · Lower complexity

More likely to search online and respond to digital channels. Decision drivers: convenience, quality, modern experience.

Google Search · Meta · Website · Email

Segment 2

Higher Complexity, Community-Anchored

Ages 70+ · Multiple chronic conditions

Less digitally engaged, higher institutional distrust. Decision drivers: trust, proximity, transportation, relationship.

Direct Mail · Community Events · Physician Referrals

Segment 3

Caregiver-Influenced Patients

Adult children driving the decision

More digitally engaged than the patient. Searching on behalf of a parent. Different intent signals and messaging needs.

Meta · Google · Caregiver Content SEO

Campaign Phases

01

Pre-Launch

8–10 weeks before opening

  • Partner with senior centers, faith communities, trusted local organizations
  • Host free health screenings, low pressure and high credibility
  • Direct mail to Medicare-eligible households within target radius
  • Google My Business fully optimized before launch
  • SEO foundation built around local high-intent search terms
  • Paid search and Meta ads targeting 65+ in defined geography
  • Physician referral relationship building begins
02

Launch

Opening month

  • Open house event: meet physicians, see the space, zero pressure
  • All Phase 1 channels active and optimizing based on early data
  • Landing pages optimized for appointment booking, minimal friction
  • Click-to-call prominent on all digital touchpoints
  • Follow-up sequence for inquiries that didn't convert
  • Budget reallocated weekly toward highest-converting channels
  • A/B testing headlines and CTAs in real time
03

Post-Launch

Ongoing optimization

  • Weekly paid media performance and CAC by channel review
  • Monthly attributed patient growth and LTV ratio analysis
  • Appointment reminder sequences to reduce no-show rates
  • Re-engagement campaign for patients who came once and didn't return
  • Physician referral network expansion
  • Patient referral program launch
  • Full funnel audit and strategy refinement for next market

Budget Philosophy & Channel Mix

40% Community & Offline Direct mail, community events, health worker activation, local partnerships. Higher trust, essential for this population.
40% Paid Digital Google Search, Meta, programmatic. Trackable, optimizable, scalable. Reallocated monthly toward actual bookings.
20% SEO & Infrastructure Website, landing pages, local SEO, content. Lower cost, long-term compounding acquisition value.
Key Attribution Setup UTMs on all digital, unique phone numbers per channel, multi-touch model, CRM source capture from day one.

Measurement Framework

Primary Metrics

  • Cost per attributed patient (CAC vs PMPM LTV)
  • Appointment booking rate
  • Show rate: booked to actual visit
  • 90-day patient retention rate

Channel Metrics

  • CPL and cost per booked appointment by channel
  • Conversion rate at each funnel stage
  • Multi-touch attribution across online and offline
  • Direct mail response rate by piece and geography

What I Would Not Measure

  • Impressions or reach in isolation
  • Website sessions without conversion context
  • Social followers or engagement rate
  • CPL without downstream conversion data
